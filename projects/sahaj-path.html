Sahaj Path is a foundational Bengali primer written by Rabindranath Tagore, designed to introduce young learners to the Bengali language in a simple and engaging manner. It holds immense significance in Bengali literature and education, serving as a timeless resource for early childhood learning. With its rhythmic prose, vivid illustrations, and progressive lessons, Sahaj Path has shaped generations of Bengali-speaking children, making language learning an enjoyable experience.

Published as a part of Rabindranath Tagore’s educational reforms, Sahaj Path has been a cornerstone of Bengali primary education for decades. Originally introduced to make language learning accessible to young minds, the book remains a fundamental tool in Bengali schools. Its literary richness and pedagogical approach have preserved its relevance, making it an integral part of childhood learning across generations.

Rabindranath Tagore

Tagore envisioned Sahaj Path as more than just a textbook—it was a creative and child-friendly approach to language acquisition. He believed that learning should be natural and intuitive, much like how children grasp language through observation and interaction. By blending storytelling, poetry, and illustrations, Sahaj Path nurtures a child’s curiosity and appreciation for Bengali culture, laying a strong foundation for literacy and creative thinking.

Tagore designed Sahaj Path with a step-by-step progression, introducing letters, words, and sentences in a natural and engaging way. Through rhythmic prose, simple stories, and beautifully integrated illustrations, the book makes learning intuitive rather than mechanical. The use of real-life scenarios helps children relate to the language, fostering both linguistic and cognitive development in an enjoyable manner.

Project Details:

Towards Development of Assistive Technology for Indian Sign Language: Cognitive Analysis and Application Development

File No.: DST/WOS-B/ET-5/2021/Sandipa(G)

The project "Towards Development of Assistive Technology for Indian Sign Language: Cognitive Analysis and Application Development" is led by Principal Investigator Sandipa Roy under the mentorship of Kuntal Ghosh at Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata. Funded by the Department of Science and Technology (DST) under the Women Scientist Scheme-B, it focuses on developing assistive tools for Indian Sign Language (ISL).

A key part of this project is adapting Sahaj Path poetry into ISL, making Tagore’s literary works accessible to the hearing-impaired. This initiative promotes inclusive education and preserves cultural heritage through sign language translation.
